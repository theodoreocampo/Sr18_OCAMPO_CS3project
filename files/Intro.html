Humans have always been naturally curious. Ever since ancient days, there were many myths/religions that are based on the planets as you could see some with the naked eye. Multiple scientists, such as Nicholaus Copernicus and Johann Kepler have contributed many laws to the science of space. Now, in the 20th century, with the invention of rockets and the multiple advancements in technology needed to explore those celestial bodies, Man’s natural curiosity has only been intensified. Many countries also raced to put the first man on the moon, which is widely known as the Space Race.

Space exploration refers to the investigation of outer space or the things beyond Earth’s atmosphere through manned or unmanned spacecraft. In short, it is humanity’s way to find out what is really out there in our Solar System, and (hopefully) the Universe too, in some way.

From the first space orbit, to possible landings on planets like Mars, and commercial spaceflight, we are really in for the ride of our lifetime!

Some famous astronauts include:

Neil Armstrong - Probably the most famous astronaut out there. He is known for taking the first steps on the Moon.

John Glenn - He was the first American astronaut to complete a full earth orbit.

Yuri Gagarin - First MAN to orbit earth.

Some famous spacecraft include:

Vostok 1 - This took Yuri Gagarin into orbit on April 12 1961. It was the first time humans had orbited space.

Hubble Space Telescope - Was put into orbit around earth on April 24, 1990. Took many photos/images that were useful in “helping unlock secrets of asteroids” and captivating the minds of people.

Apollo 11 - iconic space mission on the moon, lead to the end of the Cold war (fought by US and USSR). Mostly known for its lunar module.
